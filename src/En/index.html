Expert Management of Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) in Brussels

Do you suffer from abdominal pain, bloating, diarrhea, or constipation related to Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)? This functional digestive disorder, although benign, can severely impact your daily life. Finally, find digestive comfort thanks to a scientifically validated and globally recognized approach: the low FODMAP diet from Monash University. At Diaeta, your expert dietitian-nutritionist personally guides you through the 3 essential phases of this protocol:

  1. Phase 1 - Elimination (4 weeks): Temporary and targeted reduction of high-FODMAP foods (Fermentable Oligo-, Di-, Mono-saccharides And Polyols) to quickly calm your symptoms and establish a baseline.
  2. Phase 2 - Reintroduction (11 weeks): Structured and methodical identification of YOUR personal triggers (fructans, lactose, fructose, polyols, etc.) and your individual tolerance thresholds for each FODMAP family.
  3. Phase 3 - Sustainable Personalization: Reintegration of as many tolerated foods as possible for a varied, balanced, and tasty long-term diet. The goal is to broaden your diet as much as possible without causing symptoms, avoiding unnecessary restrictions and ensuring complete nutritional intake.

Our goal at Diaeta is to give you back control, sustainably improve your quality of life, and allow you to eat with pleasure and serenity, even with IBS.

Cardiovascular Health

Mastery of Hypertension & Cholesterol

High blood pressure and high cholesterol are major risk factors for cardiovascular diseases, the leading causes of death worldwide. A targeted nutritional approach is one of the most effective interventions to prevent and manage them.

At Diaeta, we don't just offer generic advice. We integrate scientifically validated strategies such as:

  • The DASH (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension) approach: Rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and low-fat dairy, while limiting sodium, saturated fats, and sugar.
  • The Portfolio diet: Focuses on a portfolio of foods known for their cholesterol-lowering effects, such as soluble fibers (oats, psyllium), plant sterols, soy proteins, and almonds.

Our strength lies in the personalization of these protocols. Based on your health profile, blood tests, lifestyle, and preferences, we develop a realistic and enjoyable food plan aimed at:

  • Significantly reducing your blood pressure.
  • Lowering your LDL ("bad") cholesterol level.
  • Improving your overall lipid profile.
  • Educating you for informed and sustainable food choices.

Type 2 Diabetes: Optimized Nutrition

Type 2 diabetes is a chronic disease characterized by hyperglycemia (high blood sugar), often due to insulin resistance and/or insufficient insulin production. A suitable diet is a fundamental pillar of its management, allowing for better blood sugar control, prevention of complications, and improved quality of life.

Our approach at Diaeta is scientific and highly personalized. We go beyond general recommendations to offer you a plan that takes into account:

  • Your metabolic profile: Insulin sensitivity, fasting and postprandial glucose, HbA1c.
  • Your goals: Glycemic control, weight loss if necessary, reduction of cardiovascular risk factors.
  • Your lifestyle: Food preferences, habits, level of physical activity.
  • Your medical treatment: For optimal synergy with your overall care.

We focus on proven nutritional strategies, such as managing carbohydrate intake (quality and quantity), increasing fiber, choosing healthy fats, and promoting a low-glycemic-index diet. The goal is to improve your insulin sensitivity, stabilize your blood sugar, and potentially reduce your need for medication, always in close collaboration with your attending physician.
