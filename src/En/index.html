Frequently Asked Questions

Dietitian FAQs for Brussels Patients

Everything you need to know before booking

Do I need a doctor’s referral to see a dietitian?
No referral is required in Belgium to book a dietitian appointment. If helpful, we coordinate with your GP or specialist and can work with a medical prescription when provided.
Are consultations reimbursed?
Many Belgian mutual health funds offer partial reimbursement for dietitian consultations. Amounts and conditions vary by insurer. We provide compliant receipts/attestations; please check your mutual fund for details.
How long does a session last and what’s included?
The first consultation is about 60 minutes; follow‑ups are typically 40 or 30 minutes. Sessions include a health and lifestyle assessment, body composition analysis when applicable, goal setting, and a personalized, evidence‑based plan you can apply immediately.
Do you offer video consultations?
Yes. Secure video consultations provide the same quality of care and follow‑up, with flexible daytime and evening availability. You can book them directly via /en/appointment/.
Which conditions do you treat?
We specialize in sustainable weight loss, IBS (Low FODMAP), Type 2 diabetes, and cholesterol/hypertension. Your plan is personalized and evidence‑based.
Where are the clinics located?
We consult across Brussels (e.g., Ixelles, Uccle, Woluwe, Koekelberg, Berchem‑Sainte‑Agathe). See the full list and map on Locations.
What should I bring to my first appointment?
If available: recent labs, a list of medications/supplements, medical reports, and a 3‑day food diary. Otherwise, just come as you are—we’ll guide you step by step.
What is your cancellation policy?
Please notify us at least 24 hours in advance to cancel or reschedule. Late cancellations may incur a fee; see details in Tariffs or during booking.
